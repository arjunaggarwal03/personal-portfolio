A tasting menu that earns its length. What stuck with me wasn't any single
dish. It was the choreography: the way each course reset your palate and your
attention, the deliberate silence between plates, the room engineered so the
table felt like the only one there.

The food was modernist Indian, technically absurd in the best way, but the
thing I keep coming back to is that the *experience* was the product. The
cooking was necessary but not sufficient. Pacing, room, and restraint did the
rest. A useful reminder that in almost everything, the system around the core
artifact is what people actually remember.
